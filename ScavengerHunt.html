Scavenger Hunt

1. Evidence of design for navigation without one or more of the 5 senses: The first photo demonstrates navigation without sight and the second can demonstrate navigation without sight or hearing because the light will flash or make loud noises

2. Evidence of design for forms of assistance, broadly defined: The first photo demonstrates design for forms of assistance because the painted yellow can assist people who have a hard time seeing going down stairs

3. Evidence of design for aging, broadly defined: These two photos demonstate design for aging because elderly people may have a hard time walking up stairs without support or opening doors by themselves

4. Evidence of design for young children (no parks or playgrounds): This photo demonstrates design for young children because doors may be too heavy for young kids to open

5. Evidence of design for wheeled mobile gear (chairs, bikes, strollers, etc.) Designs against access: All three of these photos demonstrate design for wheeled mobile gear because they allow for access to those who have wheeled gear like having a place to store your bike during class

6. Evidence of design with physical barriers: This phot odemonstrates design with physical barriers because while looking around the surroundings we found that there was no elevator making these stairs impossible for those who have lack of mobility

7. Evidence of design with a lack of information: This photo shows design with a lack of information because it does not tell the user what pressing the button does leading to precautionary beliefs

8. Evidence of design with an absence of help Design priorities in the built environment: The vending maching demonstrates design with an absence of help because a user with dexterity issues in their hands may have trouble pressing the button without external help

9. Evidence of design for historical specificity: This photo demonstrates historical specificity because the historical events are connected to the architecure of the area

10. Evidence of design for environmental sustainability: This photo demonstrates enviornmental sustainability because the roof garden promotes giving back the ground to which the building took away

11. Evidence of design for health and well-being: This photo is evidence of design for health and well-being because hand sanatizer promotes cleanliness

12. Evidence of design for right handed dominate world: This photo demonstrates design for right handed people because the desk is positioned on the right side
